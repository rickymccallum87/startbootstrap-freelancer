About

Web Development

I've always been a tech-obsessed tinkerer. My passion for experimenting with technology has equipped me with a wide variety of technical skills. I employ so many of them when building a website: designing visuals, programming in multiple languages, creating a smooth user experience, and more. I enjoy the challenge of each project and take pride in the knowledge that the outcome has been so useful for my clients. By providing individuals and businesses with a web presence, I enable them to engage with a wider audience.

IT Consulting

Years ago, I started a small business offering computer help to my neighbors. I would remove viruses/adware/spyware and troubleshoot problems with both hardware and software. I always offered to optimize startup speed and responsiveness by auditing the various hidden processes and services that cause hang-ups. Though the range of my services has grown, my basic commitment has remained the same. I do my best to leave customers with a system that’s a joy to use.

Med One Pharmacy

I designed and built this responsive website with Bootstrap. As directed by the client, I focused on simplicity and ease of use for the pharmacy's elderly customers. The single-page design is mobile-first and supports all display sizes. A clear call-to-action greets visitors and guides them to directions and contact information for the pharmacy's eight locations.

I compressed images, wrote custom CSS rather than bring in heavy frameworks, and used the minified version of Bootstrap delivered over a CDN. These optimizations bring the entire website under 2MB for a snappy load time even on slow or unreliable mobile data connections.

M Patricia Diaz

A dynamic, engaging introduction to M Patricia Diaz and her recently completed memoir manuscript: Forgive Me. The bold and bright color scheme is inspired by the author’s hometown of Maracaibo, Venezuela. This website is built on WordPress, which allows M Patricia to publish blog posts keeping her readers informed on the book’s progress.

Effective Altruism DC
